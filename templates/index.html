OUR STORY

Jake and Sarah met in September of 2006 in the cafeteria of Honeoye Central School. Sarah was a junior and Jake was a sophomore.

Jake had just moved to Honeoye and had only been attending the school for a few days.

During the lunch period Sarah sat with her usual group of friends (will be attending the wedding) and saw Jake a few tables over eating all alone. She has never been the new kid, as she completed her entire K-12 education at HCS. Feeling like Jake could make some new friends she went over to his table and asked him what his name was introduced herself and asked him if he would like to come sit with her and her friends who which he replied "No."… We thought the story would end there.

Honeoye Central is a very small school with only a few hundred students in attendance. You see the same faces in the hallways, cafeteria, assemblies and classes everyday. The paths of Jake and Sarah kept crossing; although they never really spoke or became friends they both knew mutual people and noticed one another from afar.

In November of 2007 Jake and his family moved away from Honeoye and Sarah would be lying if she said she didn't notice that he was gone.

In August of 2010 Sarah was searching the land of Facebook and stumbled across Jake's profile. Once again getting up the courage she clicked "add as friend". They began talking, that turned into texting, that turned into meeting up in person.

Meeting up in person turned into dates, which turned into dating. That lead to "meeting the parents" (I actually met Jakes mom on our first date!) and that lead into being what the kids call "official".

The cafeteria story wasn't brought up until about a month into dating when Sarah asked "Do you remember that day in September I asked you to sit with me?"

The Next Chapter

Jake finally did give Sarah the answer as to why he said "No" that day when she asked him to sit with her. He admits he was scared – because I was a girl.

Fast-forward to a year into dating – Sarah and Jake rented their first apartment together, a small one bedroom above a shop on Main St in Victor, NY. There they lived for 2 years until they moved to another small one bedroom apartment in Fairport, NY – where they currently reside.

They are the proud parents of two fur-babies; two tuxedo cats one named Bean the other Simon.

Over the years jobs have changed, apartments have changed but the love between them stayed the same.

The Proposal

In February of 2015 Jake showed up at Sarah’s work on Valentines Day with a bouquet of flowers and a self-designed card. The front was a picture of our Bean that read, "A Big I love you from all of the Beans in your life." On the inside of the card it instructed me that on March 14th we would have a "super special day".

She had her suspicions – The Family celebrated Kyle Houseman's (Sarah's Dad's) 50 Birthday Party on February 7rd – the Saturday before Valentines Day. After the party was over we stopped at my parents house where Jake told me "Go clean off the car, I'll be right out." My suspicion started.

I am told he asked for both my mother and fathers permission to ask me to marry him by starting out the conversation with "The time has come..." My parents told him of course he has permission and my father later told me that that was "the best Birthday ever."

On March 14th 2015 Jake and Sarah drove out to Hemlock Park a place they often frequented when she still lived at home in Honeoye. They would take pictures and just explore. Little did Jake know that about 3 feet of snow still had not melted in the park…

They both trudged through the snow up to their hips and by the time they got to the top they were both soaking wet. Jake set up the camera and the ti-pod to take some photos.

He had set the camera to start recording and then got down on one knee, asked Sarah “Will you marry me?” She waited for about 30 seconds before saying, "Aren't you supposed to say something else?" – Expecting a speech. She said, "Yes".

4 years after they first met Jake asked in a different way if Sarah would sit with him – forever.

AND NOW, WE ARE GETTING MARRIED
